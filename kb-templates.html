Create an AWS Marketplace private offer
Private offers in AWS Marketplace let you set custom pricing and contract terms for a specific buyer instead of using your public listing. In Suger, you can create and send these offers without logging into AWS Marketplace directly.
This guide walks you through creating and sending an AWS private offer from start to finish. By the end, your offer will be live and ready for the buyer to review and accept from their AWS account.
✅
Prerequisites
  • Active AWS Marketplace seller account
  • Product listed on AWS Marketplace
  • Buyer's 12-digit AWS account ID
  • Offer Manager permissions in Suger
  • AWS integration connected (Settings > Integrations)
  • Active AWS account
  • AWS account ID ready to share with you
Create the offer in Suger
Step 1: Create the draft offer
  1. 1Go to Offers in the Suger Console.
  2. 2Click + New Private Offer, then choose the AWS product to associate with the offer.
Step 2: Fill in Basic information
This section identifies the buyer and tracks the offer internally.
  • Offer Name — Name the offer so you can identify it in your pipeline
  • Salesforce / HubSpot deal — Optionally link an existing CRM deal for tracking
  • Buyer AWS Account(s) — The buyer's 12-digit AWS account ID
  • Contacts — Internal or buyer-facing people who should receive notifications
  • Internal Description — Notes for your team only. Not visible to the buyer.
Step 3: Fill in Offer terms
This section defines how long the offer is valid and the duration of the contract after acceptance.
  • Expiry Date — The deadline for the buyer to accept the offer
  • Contract Term Length — Duration of the contract after the buyer accepts
  • Future start date (optional) — Enable if the contract should begin after acceptance
⚠️
Important
Once the offer is submitted, the expiry date cannot be changed. If you need to adjust it, you'll need to withdraw the current offer and create a new one.
Step 4: Fill in Pricing information
Pricing setup depends on your AWS product type. Select the tab that matches yours.
Enter your usage dimensions and unit prices. Set the dimension API name to match your AWS listing exactly.
Set the contract price and duration. This locks in a fixed amount the buyer pays upfront or in installments.
Configure both a fixed contract commitment and a usage-based component. Use this when buyers pay a base fee plus usage overages.
Enter the markup percentage over the ISV's list price. The reseller margin is calculated automatically.
Step 5: Add legal terms
Select the agreement the buyer must accept when they take the offer: a custom EULA you upload, or the standard AWS Customer Agreement.
Step 6: Finalize and submit
Review all sections, then click Create offer.
ℹ️
Note
Suger validates the offer details before submitting to AWS. If any required fields are missing, you'll see inline error messages pointing to the specific fields.
What to expect next
✅ What happens next
  • Status: The offer will show Pending_Create. Wait up to 5 minutes and refresh — it updates to Pending_Acceptance once Suger pushes it to AWS and the buyer is notified.
  • Your next step: Open the offer, click Actions > Copy offer URL, then share the URL with the buyer so they can log in to their AWS account, review the terms, and accept.
ℹ️
Note
If the status hasn't updated after 10 minutes, go to Settings > Integrations > AWS and click Sync.

Frequently asked questions
🔖
Q: What is the correct AWS Account ID format and how do I collect it from buyers?
A: AWS Account IDs are 12-digit numbers (e.g., 123456789012). Ask your buyer to find it under My Account in the AWS console, or request it through your CRM deal record.
🔖
Q: Why doesn't my offer status match between Suger and AWS, and when will I get acceptance notifications?
A: Suger syncs with AWS every two hours, so there may be a short delay after a buyer accepts before the status updates in Suger. For the latest status immediately, go to Settings > Integrations > AWS and trigger a manual sync.
